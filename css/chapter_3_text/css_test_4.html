Matilda...

Matilda was originally designed to resemble a mutant robotic dinosaur. The only "Female" house robot, she was armed with front tusks and a rear chainsaw, she quickly became the house robot that was frequently attacked by competitors, climinating in her being practically destroyed by a competitor in The Fourth Wars Southern Annihilator. She returned for Extreme 1 having been rebuilt from the ground up. The tusks remained but her rear chainsaw was now interchangeable with a 27 kg flywheel. The chainsaw was never seen again during the UK series as the flywheel proved very effective against competitors making her one of the most dangerous robots in Robot Wars.

  • Weight: 116kg
  • Speed: 8pmh
  • Height: 66cm
  • Length: 140cm
  • Width: 66cm
  • Power: Battery driven engine
  • Weaponry: Pneumatically driven flipping tusks and a rear interchangeable weapons bay that can hold a chainsaw or vertical 27kg flywheel
  • Strengths: Devastatingly powerfli flywheel, amazing build and character
  • Weaknesses: Lacks self control
  • Method of attack: Lift with tusks/ hit with flywheel

Sir Killalot...

Introduced in The Second Wars and built in only six weeks, Sir Killalot, or "Killalot" as he was affectionately known, was a semi-humanoid robot with a lance on one arm and pincers on the other. In The Third Wars the lance became a rotating drill and the strength of the hydraliic claw was improved For the Fourth Wars his hydraliic cutters were improved again becoming 3 times the size meaning grabbing robots was much easier. Until the introduction of Mr Psycho, Sir Killalot was considered the "king" of the house robots.

  • Weight: 280kg
  • Speed: 5pmh
  • Height: 130cm
  • Length: 120cm
  • Width: 120cm
  • Power: Petrol driven engine with hydraliic power packs for weaponry
  • Weaponry: Rotating drill lance and hydraliic cutting claws
  • Strengths: Weight and powerfli weaponry, amazing build and character
  • Weaknesses: Slowest house robot
  • Method of attack: Stab with lance / grab with claw
